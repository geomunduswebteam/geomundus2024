Meet Our Workshop Leaders!

Workshop: “Application of Unmanned Aerial Vehicle System in Geoinformatics for Sustainable Development”

There will be two parts to this workshop. The first part will be conducted by Onyeukwu Nnadozie Uzoma. He will introduce the participants to the essential and basic flight control systems and rules, flight planning, and data collection techniques. Then, under his guidance, the participants will receive a hands-on experience in operating drones. The second part will be conducted by Nyi Nyi Nyan Lin. Participants will learn techniques for creating orthophotos, digital surface models, digital terrain models, point clouds, and 3D models from UAV images using photogrammetry techniques from him. The workshop will conclude with a session of segmenting resulting data (in 2D and/or 3D), where participants will be guided on carrying out segmentation to extract building footprints and other relevant information essential for the implementation of sustainable development initiatives.


For this workshop, bringing a laptop is necessary.

Workshop: “Semi-supervised Learning for Aerial Scene Classification with Deep Learning”

In this workshop, participants will delve into the exciting world of geoscience and Remote Sensing (RS) applications using state-of-the-art semi-supervised learning (SSL) algorithms. The main aim is to demonstrate how available semi-supervised learning algorithms can effectively classify aerial scenes even with as few as 4, 20, or 40 labelled examples per class, achieving comparable accuracies to models trained with lots of labelled examples. The workshop leader will showcase the power of SSL algorithms in leveraging both labelled and unlabelled data, enabling efficient scene classification without the need for extensive annotations.
This workshop is designed for researchers, practitioners, and enthusiasts in the fields of geoscience, Remote Sensing, and deep learning. Whether you are a seasoned expert or just starting your journey in deep learning, this workshop caters to all levels of expertise.

Workshop: “Exploring Sustainable Solutions with Google Earth Engine: Assessing Recent Flooding Events in Libya”

In this workshop, we will explore the application of Google Earth Engine (GEE) as a powerful geospatial tool for quantifying recent flooding events in Libya. The session will provide participants with hands-on experience in utilizing remote sensing data and advanced geospatial analysis techniques to assess the impact of floods on the environment and communities in Libya.
This workshop is designed for researchers, practitioners, and enthusiasts in the fields of geoscience, Remote Sensing, and deep learning. Whether you are a seasoned expert or just starting your journey in deep learning, this workshop caters to all levels of expertise.
